# Makgura NFT Art Asset List

## Style Direction

Makgura NFT art should feel like premium ancient-war real estate deeds and founder ownership relics: dark battlefield-black backgrounds, aged gold metal, carved stone, leather, blood-red accents, faction banners, Roman/Egyptian/Barbarian identity, and a high-end AAA fantasy MMO presentation. The collection should feel grounded, brutal, and valuable, like capital-city property rights and war-era land deeds, not generic fantasy icons.

Recommended output is square 2048x2048 PNG or WebP, with enough safe margin for marketplace card cropping. Keep typography minimal or separate from the art when possible so the website can overlay names, price, supply, remaining inventory, and metadata cleanly.

Recommended visual language:

- Founder Capital City NFTs: one-of-one capital city deed, massive city silhouette, faction crest, premium gold framing, wax/seal/deed energy, clear race identity, highest rarity treatment.
- Land Plot NFTs: capital real estate parcel, city map grid, plot boundary glow, house/foundation marker, faction-color accent, clear small/medium/large scale difference.
- Rome: imperial red and gold, marble, legion standards, colosseum/Forum architecture, organized military empire.
- Egypt: deep blue and gold, desert stone, Nile geometry, temple/obelisk motifs, trade and mystic capital identity.
- Barbarian Horde: forest green, iron, leather, bone, rough carved wood, warband banners, frontier fortress identity.
- Backgrounds: charcoal black, smoky bronze, subtle map texture, worn parchment, engraved metal, no white backgrounds.
- Consistency: every asset should belong to one Makgura collection, but each city and plot size should be instantly distinguishable.

| Plot Size | Visual Treatment |

| Medium | Wider parcel, more detail, stronger trim, courtyard/upgrade space, mid-tier real estate feel. |
| Large | Broad parcel, premium frame, estate/compound footprint, strongest glow and rarity treatment, most valuable land presence. |

## Optional Edition Treatment

The site currently treats these as unique designs with supply counts. If individual minted editions need visible differentiation later, use the same base artwork with a small edition marker layer, for example:

- Rome Small Land Plot NFT #001/150 through #150/150
- Egypt Large Land Plot NFT #01/30 through #30/30
- Barbarian Founder Capital City NFT #1/1

This keeps art production manageable while still allowing every NFT edition to feel collectible. If every minted NFT must have a unique fully rendered image instead of edition overlays, the full image count would be 603 files.
